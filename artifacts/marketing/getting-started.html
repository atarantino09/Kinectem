Getting Started

Seven steps to set up your organization

Every Kinectem org follows the same short setup checklist. Owners and admins see this same list inside the app — here's a preview of what to expect, plus a quick reference of who can do what.

  1. 1

    Upload your org logo

    Your logo shows up on every profile, team page, and game recap your org publishes — it's the face families see first. Square works best, at least 256×256.

  2. 2

    Create teams

    Teams are where everything happens on Kinectem — rosters, game recaps, highlights, the whole story.

  3. 3

    Invite coaches & staff

    Send email invites to your coaches, team managers, and front-office staff — everyone creates and owns their own personal profile. They'll help run the org right alongside you — no one person carrying it all.

  4. 4

    Add a co-admin

    Promote at least one other person to admin so the org never depends on a single person. Co-admins can manage teams, members, and posts — so things keep moving even when you're not around.

  5. 5

    Add players to a roster

    From any team page, send invites to the players on that team. Each invite starts a profile the athlete owns — and that profile follows them their whole journey. They are automatically following the org page and get tagged in team game recaps.

  6. 6

    Invite parents & guardians

    When you invite a player, you add the parent or guardian's email too. The parents can create their child's account and automatically be linked to that account. They'll be able to follow the team — and for players under 13, they'll complete the COPPA consent flow on their child's behalf. Safety built in from the first invite.

  7. 7

    Create game recaps

    This is where it all comes together. After a game, write a recap that tells the real story — who showed up, what they did, the moments that mattered. Every recap auto-tags the players in it, so each athlete's profile builds itself, game by game, season after season.

Roles & permissions

A quick reference for who can do what across the platform. Plain-language summaries, grouped by scope.

Organization roles

  • Owner — Full control. Only the owner can transfer ownership or delete the org. Can do everything an admin can.
  • Admin — Manages org details, teams (create / edit / archive), and members (invite, remove, promote other admins). Can author and approve recaps/posts for any team in the org.
  • Member — Belongs to the org but can't change settings, manage teams, or invite people.

Team roles (roster)

  • Coach — Team manager. Can edit the team, manage its roster (invite / remove players and coaches), and author recaps/posts for that team.
  • Player — On the roster. Sees team-only content and is tagged in team activity. No management rights.
  • Author — A non-coach roster member can be granted the Author position to write game recaps for that specific team, without being made a coach.
  • Game recaps — Anyone on a team's roster who isn't a player or a parent can create game recaps for that team (coaches and other non-player / non-parent roster members). No separate "author" position needed.
  • Highlights — Everyone can upload highlights, but highlights uploaded by a player or a parent must be approved by a team admin before they're visible.

Family

  • Parent / Guardian — A user with linked children. Approves follow requests, comments, and DMs for their kids, and manages COPPA consent. Guardian capability is automatic when a child is linked to your account — it's not a separate role you pick.
